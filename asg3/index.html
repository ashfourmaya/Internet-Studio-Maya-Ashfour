From the Better User Experience Through Storytelling Articles: How can framing your project in a story format help you refine your ideas? Write a paragraph explanation.: Framing a UX project in a story format helps refine ideas in several ways: it forces you to define who the user is (the protagonist), what their goal or challenge is, why they care, and how they move through the experience. That narrative structure brings clarity: it reveals missing or weak parts of your idea (scenes that don’t flow, unclear motivations, or gaps in the user journey). It helps the team align on what matters most — the emotional arc, not just features. Also, because stories are concrete, rather than abstract, they expose assumptions: once you try to imagine the story from start to finish, you hit places where you don’t really know the user’s context, what information they’ll need, when they’ll need it, etc. This lets you prune unnecessary features, prioritize what supports the core narrative, and generally sharpen the design toward a more coherent, compelling, and user-centered experience.

Creative Brief:

  • Problem: Fast fashion is contributing to massive environmental harm, overproduction, and waste. Meanwhile, countless people have clothing they no longer wear or need, sitting unused in their closets. Consumers want to be stylish and express themselves but are increasingly aware of the ethical and environmental impact of their fashion choices. There’s a gap in the market for a platform that allows individuals to rent or sell clothing directly to each other, creating a more circular, sustainable way to engage with fashion.
  • Audience: This platform targets primarily Gen Z and Millennial users (ages 18–35) who are eco-conscious, digitally savvy, and passionate about style. They care about sustainability but still want to express their identity through fashion. Many are on a budget, enjoy thrifting, and are looking for unique pieces that reflect their values. They are also interested in community-based platforms and prefer authentic, people-powered solutions over mass-produced alternatives. Secondary audiences include small-scale designers, content creators, and fashion lovers looking to share or monetize their wardrobe.
  • Competition: Direct competitors include resale and rental platforms like Depop, Poshmark, Vinted, and Rent the Runway, which focus on either secondhand sales or designer rentals. Indirect competition includes fast fashion retailers, thrift stores, and local resale through social media or marketplaces. However, unlike most existing platforms, this marketplace combines peer-to-peer sales and rentals with a strong focus on local, community-first fashion. This hybrid, ethical approach sets it apart by promoting sustainability and social connection at once.
  • Key Messages:The platform encourages users to see fashion as a shared resource, not disposable consumption. Style can be sustainable, affordable, and community-driven. Users are empowered to clean out their closets, make money, and discover unique pieces — all while helping reduce waste. Renting allows for more fashion freedom without the cost or guilt of constant buying. The key message: fashion can be circular, creative, and collaborative when it's driven by people, not corporations.
  • Call to Action: Users will be encouraged to take action through clear and empowering CTAs like: “Start Selling or Renting Your Closet Today” or “Join the Circular Fashion Movement.” Whether they want to earn from their unused wardrobe, try a new look without commitment, or support more conscious fashion habits, the platform invites them to dive in. Signing up, creating a listing, and exploring local styles will be quick, easy, and rewarding.
  • How to Measure Success:Success will be measured through both engagement and impact. Key indicators include user growth, listing activity, and transaction volume (sales and rentals). Retention rates, user satisfaction, and reviews will reflect the platform’s community strength. On a broader level, success will also be tracked by the amount of clothing kept in circulation and estimated environmental impact (e.g. CO2 or textile waste saved). Revenue from platform fees and app usage will also help evaluate long-term viability.
